Ideas for Presentations:

  • Develop ideas for an extra stanza in Poe's "The Bells" -- think of another use of bells, made from some other kind of metal, and relate it to appropriate images from the seasons or from a person's life.
  • Explore other uses of bells in poems. For example, investigate the background of the rhyme, "Oranges, Lemons say the bells of St. Clements."
  • Explore bell images in music. For example, Rachmaninoff's setting of "The Bells", Elgar's setting of Cammaerts' "Carillon" (obliquely referenced in The Nine Tailors), or Bizet's "Carillon".
  • Learn the history of the bells on the DePauw campus.
  • Research other historical bells, such as the Liberty Bell or Big Ben.
  • Learn how to ring (using cross and stretch, or some other technique) a more involved change ringing method -- this could be a group presentation, if three or four people can perform a touch.
  • Work up a solo, duet, or small ensemble handbell piece.
